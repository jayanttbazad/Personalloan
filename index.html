The Basics

What is a Personal Loan?

A personal loan is an unsecured credit facility offered by banks, NBFCs, and digital lenders. Unlike a home loan or car loan, it does not require any collateral — your creditworthiness alone secures the funds.

You borrow a fixed sum and repay it in equal monthly instalments (EMIs) spread over a chosen tenure, usually 12 to 60 months. The interest rate is fixed at the time of sanction, so your EMI never surprises you.

Personal loans are versatile: medical emergencies, travel, wedding costs, home renovation, education, or debt consolidation — lenders generally place no restrictions on end-use.

Unsecured vs Secured: Because there's no asset pledging, interest rates are higher than secured loans but approval is faster and paperwork lighter.

Key Features at a Glance

  • No collateral or guarantor required
  • Loan amount: ₹10,000 to ₹40,00,000
  • Tenure: 12 months to 60 months
  • Interest rate: 10.5% – 24% p.a. (varies by lender)
  • Processing fee: 0.5% – 3% of loan amount
  • Disbursal within 24–48 hours post-approval
  • Minimum CIBIL score: 700+ recommended
  • Prepayment allowed (charges may apply)
  • Flexible repayment via NACH/ECS auto-debit

Loan Categories

Types of Personal Loans

Different needs call for different products. Here are the most common personal loan variants available in India.

🏥

Medical Emergency Loan

Covers hospitalisation, surgery, or critical-care expenses. Many lenders offer same-day disbursals for medical emergencies with minimal documentation.

🎓

Education Loan (Personal)

Fund short courses, certifications, or overseas coaching when an education-specific loan isn't available. Amounts up to ₹10L with no institute tie-up needed.

💍

Wedding Loan

Finance venue, catering, jewellery, or honeymoon without liquidating investments. Tenure up to 5 years keeps EMIs manageable.

🏠

Home Renovation Loan

Interior upgrades, repairs, or furniture without pledging the property. Faster than a top-up home loan and available to renters too.

✈️

Travel Loan

Plan your dream holiday, international trip, or pilgrimage without draining savings. Cover flights, hotels, and visa costs upfront.

🔄

Debt Consolidation Loan

Merge multiple high-interest credit card dues or small loans into a single lower-rate EMI, simplifying repayment and reducing interest outgo.

💼

Business Personal Loan

Self-employed individuals and small business owners can use personal loan proceeds for working capital, inventory, or equipment needs.

📱

Consumer Durable Loan

Buy appliances, gadgets, or electronics with instant in-store or online financing. Some offers carry 0% interest for short tenures.

The Process

How to Apply for a Personal Loan

Most lenders now offer end-to-end digital applications. Here's what the journey looks like.

1

Check Eligibility

Verify income, age, employment status, and CIBIL score requirements before applying.

2

Compare Lenders

Compare interest rates, processing fees, prepayment charges, and customer service ratings.

3

Submit Application

Fill the form online or in-branch. Provide KYC documents, income proof, and bank statements.

4

Verification

Lender verifies documents, may call your employer, and runs a credit bureau check.

5

Sanction & Disbursal

Receive the sanction letter, sign the agreement, and funds hit your bank account—often within 24 hours.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Most lenders in India offer personal loans ranging from ₹10,000 to ₹40 lakh. ICICI Bank goes up to ₹50 lakh for select profiles. The sanctioned amount depends on your monthly income, existing liabilities, employer profile, and credit score. Typically, lenders sanction up to 20–25 times your monthly net salary.

Your CIBIL (or Experian/CRIF) score is one of the most important factors. A score above 750 typically gets you the lowest available rates and higher amounts. Scores between 650–749 may still get approval but at higher rates. Below 650, most lenders either reject the application or charge significantly higher interest. Check your score for free on the CIBIL website before applying.

In a flat rate method, interest is calculated on the full principal for the entire tenure. In the reducing balance method (also called diminishing rate), interest is charged only on the outstanding principal, which decreases with every EMI paid. As a result, the effective annual rate for a flat-rate loan is almost double the stated rate. Always compare loans on reducing balance basis.

Yes, most lenders allow prepayment or foreclosure after a lock-in period of 6–12 months. Prepayment charges typically range from 2%–5% of the outstanding principal. Some lenders (especially public sector banks) charge nil prepayment fees. Always read the loan agreement carefully for prepayment terms before signing.

Yes. Every time you apply for a loan, the lender performs a "hard inquiry" on your credit report, which temporarily reduces your score by 5–10 points. Applying to 5 lenders simultaneously signals financial distress to bureaus. Instead, use aggregator platforms to check pre-approved eligibility (soft inquiry), compare, and apply to only 1–2 lenders.

Generally, personal loan interest is not tax-deductible. However, there are exceptions: if you use the loan for home renovation, the interest portion may qualify under Section 24(b). If used for business purposes, it can be claimed as a business expense under Section 37. If used to purchase investments like stocks or mutual funds, the interest may be deductible against capital gains. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

Missing an EMI attracts a late payment penalty (typically ₹200–₹1,000 or 2%–3% of the overdue amount per month). More importantly, it gets reported to credit bureaus and can drop your CIBIL score by 50–100 points. Multiple missed EMIs can lead to your account being classified as NPA (Non-Performing Asset) after 90 days, triggering recovery action. If you anticipate difficulty, contact your lender proactively — many offer a moratorium or restructuring.
